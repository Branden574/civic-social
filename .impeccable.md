## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Politically engaged adults who want substantive civic discussion without the toxicity of mainstream social media
- **Secondary**: Policy professionals (analysts, staffers, journalists) who need a credible civic discourse tool
- **Tertiary**: General public / everyday citizens who want to stay informed and discuss issues civilly
- **Context**: Users come here to engage thoughtfully — they expect a platform that respects their time and intelligence

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Transparent, Modern** — like a civic startup that's fresh, direct, and no-nonsense. Not afraid to take a stand on quality discourse while remaining accessible.

### Anti-References
- **Not Twitter/X**: No doomscrolling feel, no rage-bait aesthetics, no visual chaos
- **Not government/.gov**: No boring bureaucratic feel, no serif-heavy institutional look
- **Not generic SaaS**: No dashboard-y feel, no cookie-cutter startup templates

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-first premium with warm neutral grays, indigo brand accent (#6366F1)
- **Tone**: Fintech-grade polish — clean, spacious, confident
- **Motion**: Purposeful spring/decel easing, staggered reveals, reduced-motion respected
- **Typography**: Inter — clean and modern, modular scale
- **Light + dark mode** supported via class-based theme switching

### Design Principles
1. **Signal over noise** — Every element earns its place. No decorative clutter, no rage amplification.
2. **Confident restraint** — Bold where it matters (brand moments, CTAs), quiet everywhere else. Let content breathe.
3. **Civic trust** — The interface should feel credible and trustworthy without being stuffy. Modern authority.
4. **Inclusive clarity** — Accessible contrast, readable type, clear hierarchy. Everyone can participate.
5. **Intentional motion** — Animations serve comprehension and delight, never distraction. Smooth, natural, purposeful.
